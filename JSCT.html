2018 North Canterbury Junior School Chess Tournament

Posted 4 July 2018

2018 Junior School Chess Tournament

North Canterbury Chess is pleased to announce our annual school chess tournament for North Canterbury school children will be held Sunday August 26.

The tournament is open to school teams comprising four members who are Year 8 or below. Only complete teams are able to be entered.

In a change from previous years, in 2018 we are running the tournament in a single day. Players will play six games; three before a lunch break, and three afterwards.

Parents and supporters are welcome to attend.

It is essential players bring their lunch to the tournament!

Where and When?

Sunday 26th August 9:30am-3:30pm
Leithfield School hall

The school is located at 64 Old Main North Rd, Leithfield (between Waikuku and Amberley), and just north of Pukeko Junction Cafe. See www.leithfield.school.nz for directions and a map.

The top team(s) will be found by aggregating the team members’ scores.

Entries are Open!

If you wish to enter a team, download and complete the PDF entry form and return to by email or post.  If submitting more than one team, please complete a separate entry form for each.

Entry fee is $20 per team. The entry fee can be deposited online:

North Canterbury Chess Account # 38 9001 0498139 00

Please use the name of the team as the reference.

If you have further questions, please contact North Canterbury Chess President, Scott Stringer, via the contact details listed above.

Notes for the Team Coach

Posted 4 July 2018

Knowledge of chess is not essential for the Coach!

A team numbers four players. All players should have a sound grasp of the rules of the game. A fifth player can be used as a reserve.

Players should be ranked by approximate playing strength, from number 1 (strongest) down to number 4 (weakest).  Players are usually able to accurately rank themselves. Choose a team captain (usually the strongest player).  If you have extra players, consider fielding an additional team but ensure your teams attend different qualifying rounds if possible so they don't eliminate one another.

Get the team to think up a name! It is helpful if your school appears in the team name (e.g. Waikuku Wolves, North Loburn Knights, etc).

Encourage the team to get in some practice.  The more practice the better!   Why not arrange a mini-tournament with neighbouring schools so that players get to experience a competitive environment?

The Coach will need to get the team to the venue ready for a 9:30am start.  The Coach should remain with the team while the games are played.

The tournament should be completed by 3:30pm. 

The Coach should ensure that each player has their lunch with them.

North Canterbury Chess will provide all the equipment for the competition. School uniforms are at the discretion of the school.
